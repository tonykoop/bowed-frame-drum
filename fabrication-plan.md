# Fabrication Plan — Bowed Frame Drum

## Tools Required (Maker Nexus)

- Slip roll (≥ 90 mm width capacity, adjustable to 450 mm OD)
- TIG welder with 1/16" (1.6 mm) ER70S-6 filler
- MIG welder (tack only)
- Angle grinder + flap disc 80-grit, 120-grit
- Press brake or bench brake (≥ 90° capacity, 25 mm length)
- Bench drill press, M4 and M5 bits
- Plasma or fiber laser cutter (for tone bar and brackets)
- Bench vise, V-block jig for cylindrical parts
- Roto-cutter / deburring tool
- Rubber mallet

## Step-by-Step Sequence

### Stage 1 — Frame Ring (slip-roll + TIG)

**Materials**: 1.0 mm CR steel sheet, 1500 mm × 115 mm stock panel

1. **Shear blank** to 1414.7 mm × 90.0 mm on power shear. Mark seam end with center-punch.
2. **Slip-roll coupon first** — roll a 200 × 90 mm test piece, measure spring-back OD,
   adjust roll gap. Target: OD = 450 mm ± 1 mm after spring-back.
3. **Roll frame blank** into cylinder. Lead the blank in square to rolls.
4. **Check roundness** with calipers at 4 positions. Adjust roll as needed.
5. **Tack-weld seam**: clamp in V-block, 4 tack welds evenly spaced along 90 mm seam.
6. **TIG butt-weld**: continuous weld from inside face, full penetration.
   Heat input: ~60 A, 14 V; travel speed ~5 mm/s. Let cool without quenching.
7. **Flap-disc**: grind seam flush on both ID and OD. 80-grit then 120-grit.
8. **Recheck roundness**: out-of-round < 1 mm.
9. **Drill tension holes**: 6× ø4.5 mm holes at 60° spacing on OD, centered at 45 mm depth.
   Use drill press + sine plate fixture to hit correct angular positions.
10. **Tap holes or install weld-nuts**: M4 weld-nuts TIG-tacked to frame OD at each hole.

### Stage 2 — Rim Reinforcement Strips

**Materials**: 1.0 mm CR steel, 2× strips 1414.7 mm × 12 mm

11. **Shear 2× rim strips** to 1414.7 mm × 12.0 mm.
12. **Roll each strip** alongside frame cylinder using same roll gap.
    Tip: roll simultaneously with frame if machine has multi-bead guides.
13. **Seat strips** against frame top and bottom edges, flush.
14. **MIG tack** at 6 positions per strip, evenly spaced.
15. **TIG continuous weld** both edges of each strip — inside and outside corners.
    Total weld length: 4 × 1414.7 mm ≈ 5658 mm (do in segments, cool between passes).

### Stage 3 — Tone Bar, Brackets, Wheel Arm (bench metal / laser)

**Materials**: 1.0 mm CR steel sheet, laser or plasma cut from nested layout

16. **Laser/plasma cut**: tone bar (400 × 25 mm + 2× grommet holes),
    2× mount brackets (30 × 25 mm + holes), wheel arm (80 × 15 mm + pivot hole).
17. **Deburr all parts** with roto-cutter and fine-grit stone. All edges: 0.2 mm chamfer minimum.
18. **Brake-form mount brackets**: 90° bend at 15 mm on press brake. Confirm 90° ± 0.5°.
19. **Brake-form wheel arm**: first 45° bend at 20 mm, then 45° return at 50 mm.
    Sequence: clamp long end, bend near end first; flip, second bend.
20. **Install grommets**: press ø14 mm neoprene grommets into ø13.8 mm holes in bracket short legs.
    Light press-fit; do not damage grommet bore.

### Stage 4 — Internal Assembly

21. **Mark bracket positions** on frame ID: 180° apart, 45 mm from either end.
22. **Rivet brackets**: 2× M3 × 6 mm blind rivets through bracket long leg into frame.
    Verify bracket short leg (grommet) faces frame interior center.
23. **Thread tone bar** through both grommets. Secure with M3 × 20 mm cap-screw + jam-nut
    at each end, finger-tight only — grommet grip provides damping.
24. **Tone bar alignment**: center bar at 45 mm depth, parallel to frame faces.

### Stage 5 — Rosined Wheel Option

25. **Drill M5 pivot hole** through frame top rim at 12 o'clock position.
26. **Install wheel arm**: M5 × 30 mm bolt through frame hole, arm between frame and large washer.
    Thumb-nut allows tension adjustment.
27. **Mount brass wheel** on M5 × 12 mm pivot bolt through arm, with needle bearing or PTFE washer.
28. **Apply rosin**: 3× passes of cake rosin on wheel edge. Warm wheel with heat gun for first coat.

### Stage 6 — Finish

29. **Sand entire frame**: 120 → 220 → 320 grit by hand. Remove all scratches, weld spatter.
30. **Finish options** (choose one):
    - Matte black rattle-can (rust-oleum self-etching primer + matte black topcoat)
    - Natural steel + paste wax (bare metal aesthetic)
    - Powder coat (off-site)
31. **Let finish cure** 24 hrs before head installation.

### Stage 7 — Head Installation

32. **Source head**: Remo Skyndeep 18" (457 mm) or cut goatskin to 460 mm OD.
33. **Wet goatskin** (if natural): soak 10 min, pat dry, stretch over frame immediately.
34. **Seat head**: drape over frame top edge, pull evenly around circumference.
35. **Install tension ring**: 430 mm OD aluminum or steel wrap-ring pressed over head and frame edge.
36. **Install 6× M4 × 30 mm cap screws** with 6× M4 weld-nuts on OD. Tighten in star pattern.
37. **Let dry / stabilize**: natural skin — 2 hrs; synthetic — 15 min.
38. **Final tune**: strike center, measure with tuner app. Target C3 (130.8 Hz).
    Tighten screws 1/4 turn at a time in star pattern until fundamental is reached.

## Inspection Gates

| Gate | Criterion | Tool |
|------|-----------|------|
| After step 8 | Frame OD 450 ± 1 mm, out-of-round < 1 mm | Calipers |
| After step 15 | Rim strips flush, no gaps > 0.5 mm | Feeler gauge |
| After step 23 | Tone bar floats freely in grommets | Manual tap |
| After step 38 | Head fundamental 130 ± 15 Hz | Tuner app |
| Sympathetic ring | Tone bar audible ring after head strike | Listen, stethoscope |

## Safety Notes

- TIG on 1.0 mm sheet: low heat input to prevent burn-through. Practice on coupon.
- Slip-roll nip hazard: never feed fingers close to the rollers.
- Grinder sparks: clear flammables, use face shield.
- Rosin: flammable dust — keep heat gun away from loose rosin.
