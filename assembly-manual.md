# Assembly Manual — Bowed Frame Drum

## Overview

This manual covers final assembly after all parts are fabricated and finished. Assumes:
- Frame ring welded, rim strips welded, all drilled, finished
- Tone bar and mounts fabricated and deburred
- Wheel arm fabricated (if using bowing option)

## Tools for Assembly

- M4 hex key (3 mm)
- M3 hex key (2.5 mm)
- Rivet gun (for M3 blind rivets)
- Rubber mallet
- Tuner app (chromatic, microphone-based)
- Calipers

## Assembly Steps

### A. Mount Tone Bar Brackets

1. Locate two bracket positions inside frame: 180° apart at 45 mm depth.
2. Align bracket long leg flush against frame ID wall.
3. Mark and drill 2× ø3.3 mm rivet holes through bracket into frame using drill jig.
4. Drive 2× M3 × 6 mm blind rivets per bracket.
   *(Photo placeholder: bracket riveted to frame ID)*
5. Verify bracket short leg (grommet boss) points toward frame center.

### B. Install Tone Bar

6. Press neoprene grommet into bracket short leg boss (ø13.8 mm hole → ø14 mm grommet press-fit).
7. Slide tone bar (400 × 25 × 1 mm) through both grommet bores from one side.
8. Center bar: equal overhang (~0 mm) at each end inside frame.
9. Thread M3 × 20 mm cap-screw through tone bar end hole into frame (or just jam-nut against grommet).
   Finger-tight — let grommet provide isolation.
   *(Photo placeholder: tone bar suspended in grommets)*

### C. Install Rosined Wheel (Optional)

10. Insert wheel arm M5 bolt through frame top rim hole (12 o'clock position).
11. Place washer against frame OD, thread thumb-nut on bolt.
12. Install brass wheel on arm pivot (M5 × 12 mm bolt through arm, PTFE washer each side, nut).
13. Adjust thumb-nut: wheel arm should pivot under 100 g fingertip force.
14. Apply rosin to wheel: 3 strokes of cake rosin, warm surface with heat gun, repeat.
    *(Photo placeholder: wheel arm assembly detail)*

### D. Head Installation

15. Lay frame flat on clean workbench.
16. Place head (goatskin or synthetic, cut to ≥ 460 mm OD) over frame top edge.
17. Work evenly: press head down over rim all the way around with thumbs.
18. Place tension wrap-ring (430 mm OD) over head, seat against frame.
19. Insert 6× M4 × 30 mm cap screws into weld-nuts. Thread finger-tight.
20. Tighten in star pattern (1-4-2-5-3-6 order), 1/4 turn per pass.
21. Strike center of head with finger. Check pitch with tuner. Target: C3 (130.8 Hz).
22. Continue tightening in star pattern until fundamental is within ±15 Hz.
    *(Photo placeholder: tension ring installed, tuner app showing C3)*

### E. Sympathetic Ring Check

23. Strike head center firmly. Listen for secondary ring from tone bar (shimmer/sustain).
24. If tone bar ring is not audible, adjust bar mass:
    - Too high: add blu-tack or tape mass at center of bar (lowers pitch toward head overtone)
    - Too low: trim 5 mm from each end with angle grinder (raises pitch)
    *(Photo placeholder: close-up of tone bar with blu-tack adjustment)*

### F. Bowing Mode (Optional)

25. Engage wheel arm by pressing inward until wheel rim contacts head at inside edge.
26. Apply downward bow stroke along wheel OD while keeping head relatively still.
27. Sustained drone should emerge in the 80–260 Hz range depending on bow speed.
28. For cleaner drone: apply more rosin and bow at slower, consistent speed.

## Adjustment Reference

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Head fundamental too low | Insufficient tension | Tighten screws 1/4 turn star pattern |
| Head fundamental too high | Over-tensioned | Loosen evenly |
| Tone bar does not ring | Pitch mismatch | Add/remove mass on bar |
| Tone bar chatters audibly | Grommet loose | Replace grommet or add thin foam backing |
| Wheel squeaks but no sustain | Insufficient rosin | Apply more rosin |
| Wheel squeaks and head muffles | Wheel pressing too hard | Back off thumb-nut |
