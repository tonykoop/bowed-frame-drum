# Visual BOM Brief — Bowed Frame Drum

## Brief for Visual BOM Render

The visual BOM for the Bowed Frame Drum should show an exploded-view illustration
of the six major subassembly groups, arranged from left to right in build sequence:

**Group 1 — Frame Ring** (center of composition): the rolled steel cylinder
(450 mm OD × 90 mm deep), with rim strips on top and bottom edges shown in
contrasting color. Six M4 tension-screw holes visible on the outer surface.

**Group 2 — Tone Bar Suspension**: two L-bracket mounts floating above/below the
frame cylinder center, with tone bar (thin flat bar, 400 mm) running between them
on neoprene grommets (shown as dark rubber rings).

**Group 3 — Rosined Wheel Assembly**: shown to the upper-right of the frame —
the double-bent arm, brass wheel (gold color), and M5 pivot hardware.

**Group 4 — Head + Tension Ring**: above the frame — goatskin or synthetic drum head
(tan/cream), with the tension wrap-ring (silver annulus) and six M4 cap screws
arranged in a star pattern.

**Group 5 — Hardware Callouts**: small bubble callouts for grommets, rivets, bolts,
jam nuts — with item numbers keying to bom.csv.

**Group 6 — Finish Note**: a palette swatch showing matte black rattle-can option
vs. natural steel option.

## Style

Use Heifer Zephyr palette: walnut (#3D2817), cedar (#B98B47), cream (#F8F4E9),
lapis (#1E3A8A). Steel parts in mid-gray. Brass wheel in cedar/gold tone.
Line-art exploded view preferred over photorealistic render for this blueprint round.

## Target Use

Capstone deck slide 3, instrument-showcase library card background, and explorer.html
image section.
